			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://www.brownstoner.com/blog/2015/09/downtown-brooklyn-grand-opera-house-history/">Past and Present: Downtown Brooklyns Grand Opera House</a> </h2>
		</div>
		<div class="meta2">
			<div class="source author"><span>by</span> <a href="http://www.brownstoner.com/blog/author/montrosemorris/" title="Posts by Montrose Morris" rel="author">Montrose Morris</a></div>
			<div class="com-count" ><a href="http://www.brownstoner.com/blog/2015/09/downtown-brooklyn-grand-opera-house-history/#comments">1 Comment</a></div>

	</header>
	<div class="entry-content">
				<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-206010" src="http://www.brownstoner.com/wp-content/plugins/lazy-load/images/1x1.trans.gif" data-lazy-src="http://cdn.brownstoner.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/09/downtown-brooklyn-grand-opera-house-history-3.jpg" alt="Downtown Brooklyn Grand Opera House History" width="1000" height="483" /><noscript><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-206010" src="http://cdn.brownstoner.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/09/downtown-brooklyn-grand-opera-house-history-3.jpg" alt="Downtown Brooklyn Grand Opera House History" width="1000" height="483" /></noscript></p>
<p><strong>A look at Brooklyn, then and now.</strong></p>
<p>Many of the grand store buildings built during the height of Downtown Brooklyns days as a pre-eminent shopping mecca are still with us. The Offerman Building, the buildings of Abraham &amp; Straus, Namms, Loesers, Woolworths and Oppenheim &amp; Collins still stand, even though all are now inhabited by new stores and businesses.</p>
<p>But if you look at old maps and photographs of the Fulton Street corridor, between Court Street and Flatbush Avenue, theres one category of businesses that is totally gone: the theaters.</p>
<p>The only remaining vestiges of Brooklyns large theater district are those around and including the Brooklyn Academy of Music  but in the hundred years between the end of the Civil War through the 1960s, they were scattered along Fulton Street and its nearby side streets.</p>
<p>Some were later movie theaters, like the Albee, the Duffield, the Fox and Loews, but a fair number were legitimate stage theaters. One of the finest of these long-gone theaters was the Grand Opera House. <a href="http://www.brownstoner.com/blog/2015/09/downtown-brooklyn-grand-opera-house-history/" class="more-link">(more&hellip;)</a></p>

<p>A year ago, our bathrooms &#8212; with plumbing ranging in age from 65 to 100 years old &#8212; were demolished to make way for all the new &#8220;guts&#8221; of the home: you know, trivial stuff like plumbing, electric and heating.</p>
<p>I was horrified by the thought of contributing otherwise useful materials to a landfill, so (in addition to sinks, mirrors and tubs) I asked our contractors to carefully salvage as many of the 1950s pink tiles as they could. We got a nice haul &#8212; about 750 four-inch squares.</p>
<p>Fast forward to now, and they&#8217;ve sat moldering away in our basement with zero game plan. Maybe I could sell them to a purveyor of all things retro? Maybe a neighbor or a reader would want them? I had no clue what their final destination would be, I was just happy to have saved them from the dumpster. And finally, like a bolt of lightning from Poseidon himself, their purpose became crystal-clear: I can actually reuse them again in this house!</p>

<p>More celebrities are migrating to Brooklyn from Manhattan.Actors Rose Byrne and Bobby Cannavale, who had been living in the East Village, paid $2,150,000earlier this month for a two-family, three-bedroom townhouse at <a href="http://marketplace.brownstoner.com/listing/NYC-Real-Estate-319565/453-warren-street-fort-greene-boerum-hill-brooklyn-ny-11217/" target="_blank">453 Warren</a><a href="http://marketplace.brownstoner.com/listing/NYC-Real-Estate-319565/453-warren-street-fort-greene-boerum-hill-brooklyn-ny-11217/" target="_blank"> Street</a> in Boerum Hill. The couple starred together in the 2014 remake of <em>Annie</em>.</p>
<p>Byrne and Cannavale&#8217;s new pad has both front and rear gardens, and a fireplace. The listing also advertises that the garden-level full-floor apartment could potentially be used as a commercial space, although with Cannavale set to star in HBO&#8217;s forthcoming series <em>Vinyl</em> and Byrne tied to the <em>X-Men</em> franchise, it seems unlikely that they&#8217;ll need the extra income from a ground-floor dentist.</p>
