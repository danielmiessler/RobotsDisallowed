<p>Streamlined and re-focused, there are four clearly defined series for different user types and needs &#8211; Made Simple, Handbook, Guru Guide and Technology Tips Guide.</p>
<p>Each series will have a consistent design with easy-to-understand memorable branding. Each issue will come with a free digital edition available through a voucher mechanic.</p>
<p><a href="http://media.futureplc.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/109/2015/02/THZ01.cover_.jpg"><img class="alignnone  wp-image-2302" src="http://media.futureplc.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/109/2015/02/THZ01.cover_.jpg" alt="THZ01.cover" width="100" height="129" /></a>         <a href="http://media.futureplc.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/109/2015/02/TGG01.cover_.jpg"><img class="alignnone  wp-image-2305" src="http://media.futureplc.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/109/2015/02/TGG01.cover_.jpg" alt="TGG01.cover" width="100" height="129" /></a>       <a href="http://media.futureplc.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/109/2015/02/TMS01.cover_.jpg"><img class="alignnone  wp-image-2301" src="http://media.futureplc.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/109/2015/02/TMS01.cover_.jpg" alt="TMS01.cover" width="100" height="129" /></a>        <a href="http://media.futureplc.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/109/2015/02/TTZ01.cover_.jpg"><img class="alignnone  wp-image-2300" src="http://media.futureplc.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/109/2015/02/TTZ01.cover_.jpg" alt="TTZ01.cover" width="100" height="129" /></a></p>
<p><strong>Made Simple Series</strong> &#8211; Targets the entry-level users in mass consumer markets. They will have 148 pages and a friendly, humanised design that will focus on mass consumer products such as the iPhone. They will retail at 9.99.</p>
<p><strong>Handbook Series</strong> &#8211; Targets the enthusiasts in mass consumer markets who want a comprehensive guide to using their new product to its absolute capacity. They will have 180 pages and will again focus on the mass consumer products such as the iPhone. They will retail at 12.99.</p>
<p><strong>Guru Guide Series</strong> &#8211; Targets the advanced users in specialist markets who want to go to the next level. They will have 180 pages and an encyclopaedia-style design that will focus on topics of interest to advanced PC users, Linux enthusiasts and businesses. They will retail at 12.99.</p>
<p><strong>Technology Tips Guide</strong> &#8211; Targets people of all skill levels wanting a dip-in/dip-out guide to popular software and hardware. They will have 148 pages and a brightly coloured &#8216;desk reference&#8217; design that will focus on topics of interest to advanced PC users, Linux enthusiasts and businesses. They will retail at 9.99.</p>
<hr />
<p><strong>Paul Newman</strong>,Group Editor-in-Chief of Future&#8217;s Technology Group, says, These new Bookazines series offer the perfect extension to what Futures core brands such as TechRadar, T3 and Linux Format already offer. Whether a novice or expert, there will be a series for all users in our core markets to ensure that they get the most from their tech.</p>
<p><strong>Nick Merritt</strong>, Head of Content and Marketing for Futures technology portfolio, says, &#8220;Our brands are leaders in the world of consumer and business tech media. This new Bookazine strategy allows us to talk directly to our core markets and offer them an extension to what they already love about our titles. Consumer tech is one of the most exciting markets in the world and by offering these premium titles, we are ensuring that consumers have the chance to make the most of their latest device.&#8221;</p>

<p><b>FUTURE DOUBLES ITS TECHNOLOGY AUDIENCE TO BECOME THE UKS LEADING TECHNOLOGY MEDIA PUBLISHER</b></p>
<p>On 6 July<b><a href="http://www.futureplc.com/">Future</a></b>announced the acquisition of <a href="http://www.netcommunities.com/"><b>Net Communities</b></a>, an award-winning digital company that specialises in advertising solutions and publishing for a range of market leading, global media partners.</p>
<p>The acquisition, for an undisclosed amount, further strengthens Futures digital reach in the UK, particularly within the technology and games markets.</p>
<p>Future currently has a UK online audience of 8m monthly users across its technology, games, film, music, photography and creative portfolio. Net Communities has a UK online audience of over 10m monthly users across a renowned portfolio of sites from publishers such as <b>Vox Media, Purch </b>and <b>Digital Trends</b>.</p>
<p>In technology, the deal solidifies Futures market leading technology portfolio and brands such as <b>TechRadar</b>, <b>T3</b> and <b>Gizmodo UK</b> will now be joined by <b>Net Communities</b> renowned portfolio.</p>
<p>These partnerships grow Futures tech-loving audience to a combined reach of over <b>15 million engaged users</b> and confirms <b>Futures Games reach as the biggest in the UK.</b></p>
<p>Not only will the acquisition see Future double its online reach in the UK, and thus provide clients with even more ways to connect with an influential audience, but it will also accelerate the companys Business Technology proposition with the acquisition of Net Communities sites<b> ITProportal, ITReviews</b> and <b>ITWhitePapers</b>which, combined with Futures B2B sites <b>TechradarPro </b>and <b>Lifehacker UK</b>, deliver an audience of over 2m IT Decision Makers.</p>
<p>Zillah Byng-Maddick, CEO of Future, says,</p>
<p><i>This acquisition of Net Communities is a great opportunity for us to leverage the synergies and specialism between the two businesses. Future has a strong track record in the tech sector connecting audiences with technology and lifestyle brands. Our combined audience reach will now ensure we are able to unlock the best commercial deals in the UK market place.</i></p>
<p>Andy Evans, Net Communities CEO and Founder, says,</p>
<p><i>There has been a long-standing mutual admiration between Net Communities and Future, so this opportunity was an obvious fit for both business. As a result, this acquisition came together very naturally enabling Future to deliver exceptional market reach across some of the most admired web properties consumer and B2B technology. The Net Communities team and I are very excited about the opportunities that can be created as we move forward in unison</i></p>
